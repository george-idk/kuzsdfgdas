Community
Canvas Bolton Town Centre

A collaborative civic art proposal for a temporary or semi-permanent painted public surface.

Public Art / Participation / Place

Paint the town together.

Community Canvas Bolton proposes a supervised collaborative painting project in Bolton town centre, inviting residents, schools, artists, youth groups and local organisations to create a shared civic artwork.

Bolton creates public space through colour.

01 / Proposal

The idea

The project would turn a selected pavement, square or prepared surface into a public canvas. Instead of a single artist producing a finished mural alone, the artwork would be built collectively through guided participation.

The aim is simple: make the town centre brighter, more participatory and more visibly shaped by the people who use it.

01

Community led

Residents and groups would contribute designs, patterns, messages and visual ideas within agreed guidelines.

02

Supervised

Organisers, volunteers and artists would manage the site, materials, flow of people and public safety.

03

Flexible

The project could be temporary, semi-permanent or linked to a wider town centre event or festival.

02 / Design System

Visual approach

Bauhaus-inspired public design

The canvas would use simple geometric forms, strong colour blocks and clear visual sections. This keeps the project bold, readable and easy for lots of people to contribute to without the final result looking messy.

Colours

  • Red for energy
  • Blue for civic identity
  • Yellow for optimism
  • Black lines for structure

Participation rules

Each participant would be given a section, prompt or shape to complete so the artwork stays coherent.

Local identity

Design prompts could reference Bolton landmarks, textile history, markets, communities and everyday town life.

Finish

The completed work could be sealed or documented depending on whether the surface is temporary or longer term.

03 / Location

Town centre site

Bolton
Town Centre

Site needs

A visible public space with safe pedestrian movement, enough room for participation and minimal disruption.

Access

The project should avoid blocking entrances, crossings, emergency access, tactile paving or key walking routes.

Permissions

Approval would be needed from the relevant landowner, Bolton Council and any highways authority if applicable.

06 / Safety

Risk and access

Surface safety

Paint and coatings should be non-slip, suitable for outdoor use and appropriate for the chosen surface.

Public movement

Clear walking routes, barriers and signage would be used to avoid crowding or obstruction.

Supervision

Volunteers would manage queues, materials, cleaning, accessibility and participant guidance.

Maintenance

The proposal should set out whether the artwork is temporary, removable, sealed or maintained over time.

Documents required

Risk assessment, method statement, materials list, site plan, permission letter and event management plan.
